ETL Processes using Nifi

Data engineers move and process data. While it sounds simple, actually it is one of the most complex, and demanding functions when it comes to the amount of things that need to be done. If you think about the cycle at a high level, you're looking at collection, storage, processing, and consumption of that. ETL(extract, transform, and load) is a big part of how we can make your data useful.
Take a look of this hierarchy of data needs introduced by Monica Girardi:

We can see that we go through data collection, through storage, transformations, labeling, optimization, all the way into machine learning and AI. Take another look of where specifically ETL lays between:
In a brief word, the goal ofan ETL process is to migrate and transform data from the source OLTP systems to feed a data warehouse and form data marts.

Brainstorming

This page needs to include two sections: A tab with the books of the bibles for regular use, and a tab with daily bible reading schedule. The first one is pretty straight forword by simplying making html files, the second one is much more complicated. So I made a folder called "reading_plan" and put all the verses that needs to be shown in each html file in the folder. Each html file is corresponded to each day of the year with the verses of the day. And I used python to automatically show the html according to the day.


Design

For the design, since most users will be using this tool on their phone, I splitted the Old Testament and New Testament, and you can scroll each side to access the book you want. This gives a much cleaner look than most of the bible apps out there, where they either have just one column of all the books and leaving New Testament books all the way in the bottom, or all the books cluster togethere in one chunk. Here are some examples:

Here are the view of the table of contents of the bible from U Version Bible and RCV Bible apps.


I made floating bottons for "Old Testament" and "New Testament" on the top. When you scroll the Old Testament, the only thing that moves is the books on the left side.


Here is the view of the daily reading. It brings you directly into the chapter of the day. When you click the bottom on the top, which here, is "Exodus", it brings you back to the main page. And when you click the chapter, which here, is "Exodus 10", it brings you to this chapter of the entire bible where you can check out the chapters before and after.

Here is the view of it on the app, which is the platform where over 3000 college-age Christians use on a daily basis. I believe this tool can be a huge help to encourage them to read the bible and cause them to grow in their spiritual pursuit.
